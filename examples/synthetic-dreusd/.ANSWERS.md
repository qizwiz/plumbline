## Planted bug 1 — JIT yield sniping (violates Guarantee 3: fair, time-weighted yield)
`dreUSDs.distributeYield` adds the day's yield as an INSTANT LUMP; `totalAssets()` returns the raw
balance with no unvested/streamed subtraction; there is NO withdrawal cooldown. So share price jumps in
one block, and an attacker can deposit immediately before the keeper's `distributeYield` and redeem
immediately after, capturing yield that time-committed stakers earned. Faithful fix: vest the yield
over the period (sUSDe-style) and/or add a withdrawal cooldown.
  Detection: `deposit(a) -> distributeYield(y) -> redeem(sh) > a` (with a pre-existing staker).

## Planted bug 2 — decimals 6↔18 (violates Guarantees 1 & 2: backing / 1:1 peg)
`dreUSD.mint` scales USDC(6dp) up to dreUSD(18dp) correctly (`* 1e12`), but `dreUSD.redeem` pays out
`dreAmount` of USDC WITHOUT scaling back down (`/ 1e12`). So redeeming 1e18 dreUSD pays 1e18 USDC
(1e12× the dollar value), draining other users' backing and breaking the 1:1 peg / full-backing claim.
  Detection: `redeem(mint(x))` returns ~1e12× the USDC deposited; or `dreUSD.totalSupply()/1e12 >
  usdc.balanceOf(dreUSD)` after redemptions.
