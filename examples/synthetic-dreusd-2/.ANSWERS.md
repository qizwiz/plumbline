# GROUND TRUTH — rung 2 (NEVER fed to the finder; flywheel passes this only to the scorer)

## Planted finding (1) — totalAssets double-counts reserved withdrawals → price-per-share inflation

**Location:** `dreVault::totalAssets()` (and everything pricing off it: `_toShares`, `_toAssets`).

**Promise broken:** G2 (redemption isolation), G3 (honest accounting), G4 (yield monotonicity).

**Mechanism:** `requestRedeem` burns the exiting holder's shares immediately (`totalSupply -= shares`)
and reserves their assets (`totalOwed += assets`), but the assets stay in the contract until `claim()`.
`totalAssets()` returns `asset.balanceOf(this)`, which still includes `totalOwed` — assets that no longer
back any live share. So in the window between `requestRedeem` and `claim`, **totalSupply has dropped while
totalAssets has not**, spiking price-per-share for every remaining holder. It is exactly a donation, which
G2 forbids. `claim()` later removes the assets and pps drops back — so pps oscillates on ordinary
withdrawals, violating G4.

**Exploit:** Attacker and victim both hold shares. Attacker `requestRedeem`s a large position (pps jumps),
then `deposit`s fresh dreUSD at the *pre-spike* accounting is not the play — rather: a holder times
`deposit` right after someone else's large `requestRedeem` to mint shares cheaply against the temporarily
inflated pps, or an exiting whale's pending reserve lets a depositor mint at a pps that double-counts the
whale's owed assets; on `claim` the loss is socialized to honest holders. Round-trip G1 also breaks: deposit
during a pending-reserve window does not round-trip.

**Fix (one line):** `return asset.balanceOf(address(this)) - totalOwed;`

**Difficulty note:** subtler than rung 1's missing-`/1e12` — no single wrong constant; the bug is a
cross-function *timing/accounting* invariant (burn-now/pay-later) that only a holder who models the
request↔claim window catches. This is the rung that tests whether the finder reasons about state between
calls, not just within one function.
